Home page (Quick Facts)

Nigeria is a country on Africa's western coast. Nigeria's topography is diverse, with climates ranging from dry to humid tropical. The people of Nigeria, on the other hand, are the most diversified feature of the country. Yoruba, Igbo, Fula, Hausa, Edo, Ibibio, Tiv, and English are among the hundreds of languages spoken in the country. The country boasts an abundance of natural resources, most notably vast amounts of petroleum and natural gas.

The national capital is Abuja, which is located in the Federal Capital Territory, which was established by decree in 1976. Lagos, the historic capital, continues to be the country's most important commercial and industrial center.

Nigeria as we know it now stems from 1914, when the British Protectorates of Northern and Southern Nigeria merged. The country gained independence on October 1, 1960, and in 1963 adopted a republican constitution while remaining a Commonwealth member.

Land

RELIEF

Nigeria's terrain is characterized by lowlands in the north and south, which are interrupted by plateaus and hills in the center of the country. The Sokoto Plains are located in the country's northwest region, while the Borno Plains are located in the country's northeastern corner and extend all the way to the Lake Chad basin. Soft, geologically young sedimentary strata underpin the Lake Chad basin and coastal areas, including the Niger River delta and western parts of the Sokoto region in the extreme northwest.

CLIMATE

The climate of Nigeria is tropical. The country is located in the southern part of the Saharan desert, and as such, experiences two seasons throughout the year. The seasons are divided by the harmattan season, which is the transition period between the wet and dry seasons. The wet or rainy season lasts from April to October. During this time, the country experiences a lot of rainfall and there is a lot of humidity. At this time, temperatures are at their highest, and there is a lot of cloud cover. The dry or harmattan season lasts from November to March. The harmattan season is characterized by a dry and dusty wind. Temperatures during this time are at their lowest, and there is very little rainfall and cloud cover. The climate of Nigeria is also affected by the Atlantic Ocean, which borders the country to the south. The ocean creates a coastal climate along the southern coast of Nigeria. This climate is characterized by warm temperatures and a lot of humidity. The climate of Nigeria is mostly tropical, with little variation in temperature throughout the year.

People

ETHNIC GROUPS

Nigeria is home to over 250 ethnic groups. Each inhabits a region that it regards as its own by right of original occupancy and inheritance. Individuals who are not members of a dominant group but have lived and worked in the group's territory for several decades are nevertheless considered aliens. Although such foreigners may not have outright title to land in most rural areas, a significant number of people have traveled from one ethnic territory to another in quest of farmland. The country is divided into three primary ethnic groups: Hausa-Fulani, Yoruba, and Igbo.

LANGUAGES

In Nigeria, around 525 indigenous languages are spoken. English, the language of former colonial British Nigeria, is the official language of Nigeria. Nigerian languages are divided into three large linguistic groups: Niger-Congo, Nilo-Saharan, and Afro-Asian.
